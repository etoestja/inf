Global Positioning System

Problem

Get precise position of an object on Earth

Previous solutions

Sailors used sextants.

  • Accuracy only ~500 meters
  • Depends on weather
  • Complex

Solution

  • Ground-based systems (1940s, World War II, GB)
  • Sputnik (USSR, 1957), Doppler effect, Americans
    \(\frac{f}{f_0}=\sqrt{\frac{c+v}{c-v}}\)
    ⇒ location ⇒ inverse problem
  • First satellite system im 1960s

GPS

  • Implemented in 1990s
  • Military at first
  • Accuracy ~10 meters
    (\(\times\,50\uparrow\)!)
  • 32 satellites in space
    (24 originally)
  • Control station
  • Four ground antennas for sync

Satellites (space segment)

  • 24 satellites
  • 6 planes
  • circular orbits
  • \(\geqslant\) 6 visible from anywhere

How?

basic principle

  • Satellite transmits time and its position
  • \(\begin{cases}|\vec{x}-\vec{x_0}|=c(t-t^i_0)\\\end{cases}\) to get coordinates
  • Doppler effect to measure velocity more accurately
    \(\frac{f}{f_0}=\sqrt{\frac{c+v}{c-v}}\)

Applications

    Military (missiles), planes, ships, car navigators, robots, games
