Problem

Get precise position of an object on Earth

Previous solutions

  • Sailors used sextants
  • Ground-based systems (1940s, World War II, GB)

Drawbacks: inaccurate or complex

History of GPS

  • Sputnik (USSR, 1957), Doppler effect to measure its velocity (inverse problem)
    \(\frac{f}{f_0}=\sqrt{\frac{c+v}{c-v}}\)
    ⇒ velocity 
  • GPS Fully implemented in 1990s
  • Military at first

How?

basic principle

  • Satellite transmits time and its position
  • Trilateration: device solves \(\begin{cases}|\vec{x}-\vec{x}^i_0|=c(t-t^i_0),\,i\in\overline{1,N}\\\end{cases};\vec{x}\in R^3\)
    to get coordinates
  • Doppler effect to measure velocity more precise
  • need \(\geqslant 4\) satellites to determine position

How?

Satellites

  • 24 satellites
  • 6 planes
  • circular orbits
  • \(\geqslant\) 6 visible from anywhere

How?

Sync

  • Satellites must be synced with each other
  • GPS has ground stations for that

Qualities

  • Precise location by simple and cheap methods
  • Accuracy ~10 meters (\(\times\,50\uparrow\)!)
  • Better accuracy for military purposes

Applications

    Military (missiles), planes, ships, car navigators, robots, games

Summary

  • Other ways are inaccurate or complicated
  • Idea: USSR, Sputnik, Doppler effect
  • Military purposes at first, established in 1990s
  • Ground stations and satellites
  • Widely used nowadays
